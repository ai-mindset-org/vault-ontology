What This System Does

It Collects

Every transcript from Krisp, every voice note, every web clipping, every AI conversation, every journal entry, every Linear task snapshot — all flow into raw-data. This is the evidence layer. Once something enters here, it never changes. It's geological — sediment that accumulates, immutable and timestamped.

~15,000 files · transcripts/ · voicenotes/ · Clippings/ · AI chats/ · _journal/ · Linear Issues/ · _sessions/

It Processes

A six-phase multi-agent pipeline extracts meaning from raw inputs. Binary files become text. Four domain observers run in parallel — examining people, products, operations, and market signals. A change detector catches contradictions with existing facts. A merge judge deduplicates and resolves conflicts. Then three writers update the output layers, each staying strictly within its boundary.

Phase 0: extraction · Phase 0.5: multimodal · Phase 1: 4 observers · Phase 1b: reconciliation · Phase 2: 3 writers · Boundary check

It Visualizes

This dashboard is the living map. It shows where information lives, how it flows, and where the bottleneck is. 78% of the vault is raw evidence that has never been processed into stable knowledge. The pipeline exists to close this gap — to turn 15,000 recordings and conversations into facts you can act on.

"Every fact needs a source. Every source is immutable. Every synthesis has an expiry date."

raw-data/

"What evidence do we have?"

The sediment layer. Everything that enters the vault as a verbatim record of something that happened. Transcripts of calls. Voice recordings made on walks. Articles clipped from the web. Conversations with AI models. Daily journal entries. Screenshots. Task snapshots from Linear.

This layer has one absolute rule: immutability. Once a file enters raw-data, its content never changes. You can tag it, link to it, extract from it — but never edit the original. It's your geological record.

Signals: timestamp in filename (YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM), tags like {transcript}, {krisp}, {clip}, {voicenote}. Content is a capture, not a creation.

knowledge/

"What is true?"

The crystal layer. Facts that have been verified, synthesized, and stabilized. Team member roles. Partner profiles. Company rules and processes. Product definitions. Strategic documents. Published articles. Research outputs. The 12,000+ atomic notes — quotes, insights, principles — that form the bedrock of understanding.

Every knowledge entry carries provenance: which raw-data it came from and a confidence level (high, medium, low). Knowledge is stable over weeks and months — it only changes when truth itself changes.

Three sub-domains: People (roles, partners, curators, authors), Company (rules, processes, products, labs, strategy, offers), Domain (articles, research, PKM, self-knowledge, atomic notes).

engagement/

"What happened between us?"

The pulse layer. Every interaction — calls, meetings, partnership touches, lab activity, exit interviews — recorded as a relationship event. This isn't a fact about the world (knowledge) or a task to do (working-memory). It's the heartbeat of connections.

CRM partner cards track progression through stages. Meeting notes capture agreements and action items. Lab cohort data shows who showed up, who submitted homework, who is engaged. Exit interviews reveal what worked and what didn't.

This layer answers: when did we last talk? What was agreed? How is the relationship trending? It's the operational CAIO record — Chief AI Officer's interaction log.

call-logs partner-touches lab-activity crm-state exit-interviews

working-memory/

"What to do next?"

The flame layer. Bright, volatile, designed to burn out. Daily focus sentences that expire at midnight. PM analyses that matter for one or two weeks. Draft messages that vanish once sent. Planning docs that expire after the event. Active context for agents that becomes stale when project state changes.

This is the smallest layer by design — only ~200 files at any given time. If working-memory grows too large, it means decisions aren't being made. The flame should burn hot and fast, not smolder.

Decay rules: focus (1 day), drafts (until sent), analyses (1-2 weeks), planning (until event), context (until state changes), tasks (until done).

Rules of the System

Six constraints that keep the ontology honest

Rule 01
Provenance
Every fact carries its birth certificate: source: raw-data/... and confidence: high | medium | low. No orphan claims.
Rule 02
Boundary
One agent per layer. The agent that extracts never synthesizes. The agent that writes knowledge never touches working-memory. Separation of concerns at the epistemological level.
Rule 03
Naming
{date}-{slug}.md — every ontology file carries its birthday. No ambiguity about when a fact was established.
Rule 04
Immutability
raw-data is carved in stone. Append metadata, add links, extract facts — but never modify the original content. The evidence must remain intact.
Rule 05
Flow
Information flows in one direction: raw-data → knowledge → working-memory. No shortcuts. No layer skipping. Evidence before truth. Truth before action.
Rule 06
Decay
Working-memory has an expiry date. Daily focus dies at midnight. Drafts die when sent. If working-memory keeps growing, decisions aren't being made.
